Life Physics
Honours Life Physics, Biophysics Specialization
Printable Version

Advisors: See Faculty of Science, academic advisors.

The Biophysics Specialization increases the depth and breadth of physics and biology courses at an honours level and prepares students for graduate-level study or work in industry. This specialization includes the requirements for the Honours Life Physics program, with the following additions and substitutions:

  • BIOL 308 and five BIOL elective courses at 300-level or higher
  • PHYS 234, PHYS 263, PHYS 358, PHYS 359, and one PHYS elective course at the 300-level or higher
  • MATH 114, MATH 227, and MATH 228
  • PHYS 242/PHYS 242L in place of PHYS 224
  • PHYS 121 and PHYS 122 in place of PHYS 111 and PHYS 112

Continuation in Honours Life Physics, Biophysics Specialization requires a cumulative overall average, Physics average, and Biology average of 60%.

In order to graduate with Honours Life Physics, Biophysics Specialization, the following requirements must be successfully completed:

  1. 21.75 units that include:
    • 8.0 PHYS units: PHYS 121, PHYS 131L, PHYS 122, PHYS 122L, PHYS 225, PHYS 234, PHYS 242, PHYS 242L, PHYS 256, PHYS 256L, PHYS 263, PHYS 280/BIOL 280, PHYS 358, PHYS 359, PHYS 380, PHYS 395, PHYS 396, and PHYS 491
    • 2.5 MATH units: MATH 114, MATH 127, MATH 128, MATH 227, and MATH 228
    • 3.0 CHEM units: CHEM 120, CHEM 120L, CHEM 123, CHEM 123L, CHEM 237, CHEM 237L, CHEM 266, and CHEM 266L
    • 2.25 BIOL units: BIOL 130, BIOL 130L, BIOL 239, BIOL 273, and BIOL 308
    • 0.5 ENGL unit: ENGL 193/SPCOM 193
    • 5.5 elective units distributed as follows:
      • 0.5 PHYS lecture unit 300-level or higher
      • 2.5 BIOL lecture units 300-level or higher
      • 2.5 units chosen from any subject
  2. Enrolment in PHYS 10 when offered.

Notes

  1. Students should consult an academic advisor and the Life Physics web page for suggested sequencing of the required courses.
  2. PHYS 111L may be substituted for PHYS 131L.
  3. PHYS 132L may be substituted for PHYS 122L.
  4. A 100- or 200-level BIOL course may be substituted for BIOL 273.
  5. STAT 202 may be substituted for PHYS 225.
  6. PHYS 437A (for one-term research projects) or PHYS 437A/PHYS 437B (taken over two terms) or BIOL 499A and BIOL 499B (taken over two terms) may be substituted for PHYS 491.
  7. Students wishing to purse graduate studies (including areas in biology, physics, or biophysics) should consult with a Physics advisor and a graduate officer in order to choose electives that provide them with the desired background for their choice of graduate program and/or school.

Suggested Electives for the Biophysics Specialization

Prerequisites to a number of fourth-year physics courses: PHYS 334, PHYS 342, and PHYS 364.

Interest in Experimental Methods

2.0 units from the following: BIOL 335L, BIOL 361, PHYS 131L (in place of PHYS 111L), PHYS 132L (in place of PHYS 122L), PHYS 236, PHYS 260L, or PHYS 391/PHYS 391L

Interest in Theoretical Methods

2.0 units from the following: BIOL 364, BIOL 382/AMATH 382, PHYS 236, PHYS 334, PHYS 364, PHYS 365, PSYCH 420, SYDE 544, or SYDE 584 (if BIOL 273 not yet completed)

Interest in Biophotonics

PHYS 364, PHYS 342, and 1.0 additional unit from the following: PHYS 391/PHYS 391L, PHYS 393, or PHYS 394

Interest in Microbiology

BIOL 240/BIOL 240L, BIOL 241, and 2.0 additional units from the following: BIOL 345, BIOL 346, BIOL 348L, BIOL 431, BIOL 442, BIOL 443, BIOL 444, BIOL 447, BIOL 448, or BIOL 449

Interest in Environmental Sciences 

BIOL 150, BIOL 350, BIOL 359, and 1.5 additional units from: BIOL 351, BIOL 354, BIOL 361, BIOL 383, BIOL 450, BIOL 452, BIOL 455, BIOL 456, BIOL 457, BIOL 462, BIOL 470, BIOL 479, BIOL 488, or PHYS 364

Interest in Molecular Biology and Biotechnology

BIOL 309, BIOL 342, BIOL 432, and 1.5 additional units from the following: BIOL 331, BIOL 335L, BIOL 434, BIOL 483, or BIOL 484
